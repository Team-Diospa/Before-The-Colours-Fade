# Overarching Narrative Structure and Narrative Design

This document details the story pacing, thematic delivery, and mechanical integration for "Before the Colours Fade", focused on maintaining a lingering mystery, existential absurdism, and psychological horror from the player's perspective.

## 1. Pacing the Mystery (Avoiding Forced Drama)

Rather than forcing heavy-handed tragedy or explicit plot points early on, the narrative focuses on a subtle, lingering emptiness. The player should experience Chapter 1 from Hilbert's perspective, where the conflict feels like normal daily struggles but with an underlying weirdness.
- **Act I (The Grey Morning):** Hilbert goes through mundane apartment and classroom routines (sleeping in, checking doodles, dealing with peers, prepping for the quiz). The mood is grey and quiet. No tragedy is explicitly mentioned. The emotional weight is presented naturally through quiet moments and lingering sounds.
- **Act II (The Dream Shift):** The dream world is a high-fantasy deckbuilding environment. It represents a warm, comfortable escape from the cold classroom, letting the player experience the direct contrast.
- **Act III (The Reality Anomalies):** During combat, shifting back to reality introduces weird existential absurdism. The player encounters frozen moments (like a paused classroom) and bizarre manifestations (like a paper monster demanding test answers).
- **Act IV (The Lingering Ending):** Resolving the combat yields a quiet mystery. Hilbert wakes up in a silent classroom with a completed quiz, a blank reminder on his phone, and a lingering warm sensation. This leaves the player intrigued rather than delivering a premature resolution.

## 2. Existential Absurdism and Relatability

To prevent the narrative from feeling excessively edgy, the horror is balanced with mundane humor and absurdism:
- **Absurd Combat Scenarios:** Battling a monster made of exam papers using sketch-pad swords and spelling cards.
- **Relatable Friction:** Peers react with standard student annoyance or concern, making the interactions feel natural.
- **No Melodrama:** The dialogues focus on simple sentences, leaving the heavy lifting to the silent gaps and the color transitions.
