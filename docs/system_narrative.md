# Overarching Narrative Structure and Narrative Design

This document details the story pacing, thematic delivery, and mechanical integration for "Before the Colours Fade", focused on maintaining a light, lingering mystery and absurdist humor during Chapter 1.

## 1. Chapter 1 Tone: Lingering Mystery (Avoiding Heavy Tragedy)

To prevent the narrative from feeling predictable or forced, Chapter 1 avoids explaining the backstory. The truth remains entirely hidden, focusing instead on school stress and weird occurrences.
- **Act I (The Grey Morning):** The player deals with everyday student stress (sleeping in, studying, peer friction). The mood is mundane but carries a slight, unexplained spacing-out quality.
- **Act II (The Dream Shift):** The transition to the child world feels like a typical high-fantasy deckbuilder. The player is encouraged to accept this escapism because it is vibrant and has clear goals (defeating monsters).
- **Act III (The Cracks in the Dream):** During battles, the player is forced to shift back to the grey classroom. This reality check introduces discrepancies (the monster shouting about exam answers).
- **Act IV (The Lingering Ending):** The chapter ends with a quiet question mark rather than a tragic reveal. Waking up to find a notebook with a smudged signature next to them creates a sense of missing context without detailing any tragedy.

## 2. Foreshadowing and Absurdist Symbolism

Instead of giving away the plot, the exploration interactions drop odd, slightly absurdist crumbs:
- **The Cabbage Photo:** In the apartment, the player sees a framed photo of a boy holding a giant cabbage wearing a tie. It is a funny, relatable child memory that raises minor questions without forcing sorrow.
- **The Smudged Blueprint:** Waking up at the end reveals a drawing of a rocket-powered toaster signed 'H.H. & ...' with the second name smudged, hinting at a lost partner without spelling out memory erasure.
- **The Companion's Name:** The robot companion is named n.n. (Nomen Nescio, name unknown). It speaks with child-like enthusiasm, acting as a strange lingering invention from Hilbert's drawings.

## 3. Absurdism, Humor, and Relatability

The horror is kept subtle and psychological, balanced by grounded school humor:
- **Absurd Combat Scenarios:** Fighting a giant monster made of exam papers arguing that the answer to question four is not 'banana'.
- **Peer Relatability:** Peer 1 joking about Hilbert leaving his notebook under the cafeteria microwave, and Peer 2 telling Hilbert to stop staring at her cheese crackers.
- **The Bed Choice:** The choice to sleep in is framed around the universal desire to avoid a Monday morning test, keeping the tone grounded and relatable.

## 4. Avoiding Forced Emotional Weight

The game avoids tragic monologues, references to accidents, or forced tear-jerkers. The weight comes from silence and small details rather than direct explanations. By keeping the dialogue simple and dry, the lingering mystery feels natural and allows chapter transitions to remain open-ended.
