# Overarching Narrative Structure and Narrative Design

This document details the story pacing, thematic delivery, and mechanical integration for "Before the Colours Fade", focused on maintaining mystery, foreshadowing, absurdism, and psychological horror from the player's perspective.

## 1. Pacing the Mystery (No Sudden Spoilers)

The story is designed as a series of small, seemingly trivial puzzle pieces. The game never directly explains the tragedy or explicitly states that Hilbert has memory loss. Instead, the player must piece together the narrative themselves from the environment and characters.
- **Act I (The Grey Morning):** The mood is mundane and quiet. The player experiences school stress (sleeping in, a messy desk, a dusty guitar). The tension is low and grounded in reality.
- **Act II (The Dream Shift):** The transition to the child world feels like a typical high-fantasy deckbuilder. The player is encouraged to accept this escapism.
- **Act III (The Cracks in the Dream):** During battles, the player is forced to shift back to the grey classroom. The anomalies in the dream connect back to classroom stressors (exam instructions, handwriting).
- **Act IV (The Resolution):** The demo ends on a quiet realization rather than a melodramatic climax. A scratch on an empty desk and a nameless phone contact named n.n. leave the player with a mysterious, unsettling feeling.

## 2. The Nine Puzzle Pieces of Chapter 1

The narrative relies on nine subtle clues scattered throughout Chapter 1:

1. **The Blanket (Bed choice):** Hilbert's reluctance to wake up is presented as a normal Monday morning exhaustion, rather than an existential crisis.
2. **The Smudged Photo (Desk choice):** A photograph of two boys holding a trophy is smudged. Hilbert assumes he spilled tea on it, providing a natural excuse for why he cannot see the other face.
3. **The Guitar Tuning (Guitar choice):** Hilbert notes the guitar is tuned to open D, a tuning he doesn't play. It hints at a previous player without stating it.
4. **The Workshop Lights (Peer 1 choice):** Peer 1 mentions finding Hilbert's notebook in the workshop on 4th street and seeing the lights on yesterday. Hilbert is confused because he hasn't been there in months.
5. **Humming the Chord (Peer 2 choice):** Peer 2 is annoyed that Hilbert keeps humming a specific chord under his breath, hinting at his internal spacing out and connection to the music.
6. **Messy Marginalia (Locker choice):** The locker contains old solar-powered bicycle blueprints. Hilbert notes messy handwriting in the margins that he can barely read, indicating a partner.
7. **Exam Monster Commands (Paper Monster choice):** Instead of speaking about grief, the Paper Monster yells creepily in exam commands: "REDUCE TO SIMPLEST TERMS. ELIMINATE THE FRACTIONS. ERASE THE REMAINDER."
8. **Dissociative Paper (End of Battle):** Hilbert wakes up to find the exam fully completed in his own handwriting, but has no memory of solving the questions.
9. **The Scratch & Phone (Ending):** An empty, dusty desk next to Hilbert has the initials "H.H. + L.G. 2024" scratched on the wood. His phone contains a caller ID with a number but no name, reading simply "n.n.".

## 3. Balancing Psychological Horror with Absurdism

The narrative uses absurdist humor and school relatability to balance the psychological horror and prevent the tone from becoming overly edgy:
- **Absurd Combat:** Using a pencil-sword and drawing-pad shields to fight a paper monster representing school anxiety.
- **Peer Friction:** Classmates reacting with mundane annoyance or casual concern rather than dramatic pity.
- **B-Flat Grade:** The humor of passing the quiz with an unexplained completed paper.

## 4. Design Guidelines for Future Chapters

- **Show, Don't Tell:** Never use terms like "medication", "depression", "tragedy", or "accident" directly. Use objects (smudges, dust, handwriting) to imply history.
- **Player Deduction:** Leave the conclusions open. Let the player feel the unease of memory loss by experiencing the gaps in Hilbert's mind themselves.
