General Strategies
Tips & Tricks

    Interface
        There is an "organise" button you can click to reorder your hand
        [Right-Click] a card to instantly play it
        Dragging with [Left-Click] to play cards can still be advisable
        - Notably when there are too many cards, that you might accidentally misclick 

    Skills
    - Some skills are nice to have regardless of builds, so they won't be listed explicitly later
        Double: Mainly for duplicating the highest damage card, often for 100+ extra damage
        Clock Freeze: Especially good for freezing D-rex and delaying Bulk's sealing effect
        Reload & Speed Charge: Allows you to reset a card, e.g. cost & sealed status
        Overheat: Gives you extra cards, in case you miscount and unable to play cards on hand 

    Fragile
        Fragile cards are automatically destroyed when stay in your hand for > 1 turn
            When you play a card with a "select" effect, e.g. Against the Grain, you can see the Fragile cards flashing red, indicating they would be destroyed right after
            The in-game guide is somewhat confusing about which situation a Fragile card would be destroyed. I recommend just ignoring it altogether
        After a Fragile card is drawn, these are the possible outcomes:
            You play the Fragile card directly
            You play another card instead, so the Fragile cards are destroyed
            You discard the about-to-be-gone card, e.g. with Ferocious Claw
            You return cards back to deck to preserve the Fragile cards, e.g. with Transcode
            You play All-in, and all cards on hand would no longer be Fragile
            - This includes the newly drawn cards from playing All-in as well

    Upgrade Chips
        Upon clearing BIAS-6 difficulty, you can now upgrade cards with these chips
        Certain cards might be best left not upgraded
        - Transcode becomes Fragile when upgraded, not worth it IMO
        - Backdoor can draw 2 more cards when not upgraded
        - Homeward Bound can draw more when not upgraded
        When grinding chips, you can skip certain bosses if you're having trouble
        - I usually skips the 4th boss, since his sealing ability is annoying to deal with
        The not-D-rex drops 50 chips, instead of the usual 15 chips from other bosses 

Common Playstyles
This section covers the general ideas of how most builds are able to play most of their cards.

0-cost Playstyle

    Draw as many cards as possible, e.g. with high-cost cards
    Use cards like Oxalis's Prayer+ to set all costs to 0
    Now you can play all your cards even when deck is empty!

.
Iron Coin Playstyle

    Discard any Iron Coin+ you see, e.g. with Ferocious Claw or Genetic Mutaiton
    Use All-In+ to de-fragile Iron Coin when there are too many of them on hand
    - This allows you to discard them over serveral turns in case you couldn't keep up
    Eventually, your deck should have many Iron Coin
    - This means you don't have to worry about playing high-cost cards as much

Universal Combos
I also want to mention a few universal combos in this section. On their own, they don't really deal much damage, but they can be a good way to complement your deck if you have spare slots and not sure what to do with them.

Re-Search Thunderclap : Guaranteed Draw

    Get the Re-Search skill
    Use it for Thunderclap+ if you didn't draw it first
    Keep using it until you've drawn all the setup cards needed

This setup ensures you can draw all cards regardless of your starting hand, even if your deck consists of only low-cost cards. And when you're done drawing cards, you can easily transition into 0-cost Playstyle, where you can use Oxalis's Prayer to reduce the drawn cards' cost all to 0.

Backdoor Double : Draw, Stalling, Infinite

    Get the Double skill
    Use the skill to duplicate Backdoor
    When ready, use Tea Ceremony+ on Backdoor
    The 0-cost Backdoor can now be duped indefinitely

This combo basically lets you do infinite combo with Backdoor instead of Axiom's Core. You can pair it with Electric Discharge or Procyon Device for automatic damage if you don't mind cheesing the fight. But even if you don't, this can still work well at the beginning of the battle for drawing all your cards without expending any, as well as stalling out any Seal effect from Bulk.

I use it in my Infinite Discharge build.

Axim's Core Duplication : Extra Cards

    Use Homeward Bound+ whenever it appears
    - Use it on Axim's Core+ whenever possible
    When running out, use Tea Ceremony+ on Axim's Core
    When out of Core, you should have more cards than what you started with

Axim's Core basically lets you rewind your deck, plus duplicating 4 cards randomly into your hand. Ideally, you can repeat the cycle indefinitely when Homeward Bound and/or Axim's Core are being duplicated, but the chance is somewhat low so it's not very reliable.

That said, even if you are not using it for the infinite combo, this can be a good way to generate more copies of the useful cards in your deck, such as Laevatein.

Axim's Core Flush : Infinite

    Draw and play/discard most of the irrelevant cards
    - You can use the "Guaranteed Draw" combo above
    Make sure only these cards are remaining
    - [2] Axiom's Core (one of which is 0-cost)
    - [1] Tea Ceremony
    - [1] (any card of choice)
    - [1] (any card of choice)
    Play the 0-cost Axiom's Core
    - Now the remaining 4 cards should 100% be duplcated
    Play the 2 cards you've chosen, then reduce Axiom's Core to 0-cost before playing it
    Repeat until the enemy is dead

This strategy works with most other cards you choose, assuming it's not Fragile, and both chosen cards aren't above 4-cost. It basically ensures Axiom's Core is always duplicating the same 4 card, but the process feels cheesy, slow, and just not very fun.

I used it in my Infinite Reckless Plan build.
Building Your Deck
Categorising Cards by Purpose
Basic Idea
A casual build usually consists of these things:

    Cards for dealing damage
    Cards for buffing damage
    Cards for reducing costs
    Cards with high-cost

(1) is self-explanatory since the whole goal is to defeat bosses by dealing enough damage, and similarly for (2) since unbuffed cards often do not have enough damage capability.

(3) allows you to play cards even when you don't have many cards left in the deck, and (4) allows you to draw more cards at the early part of a battle. Both are important in allowing you to play cards in the order you want.

Some cards also fall into multiple category at once, and there can be exceptions of course. For example, the Re-Search skill allow you to always pick 1 card from deck, meaning you can always use Thunderclap+ at the start of each battle to draw basically your entire deck at once, negating the need to include (4) in your build.

Example Build
My Dew build follows this pattern too:

    Clearing Fog, Assassin's Sign
    Dew Sharpening, Sublimation
    Oxalis's Prayer
    Heat Burn

These cards are the core of my build. You can include other cards of course, like how Backdoor can let you duplicate more cards per battle, and Nelker's Experiment for drawing/buffing in general. Experimenting things yourself is part of the fun!

Real-time Attacker
At lv1, D-rex already has the ability to insta-kill you if you don't deal certain amount of damage in real-time. Dealing 40 damage would stun it for a few seconds, after which 40 more damage needs to be dealt for the next stun.

At lv4, each time it wakes up, namely at the start of battle and after recovering from stun, all cards on hand have their cost +1. This is particularly detrimental when your hand is all 0-cost with an empty deck. One way to avoid this is by waiting for its wake before reducing card costs.

It's worth noting that it does get stopped completely by the Clock Freeze skill, eliminating the real-time factor (until 3 cards are played of course.) Use the frozen time to come up with a good sequence to play your cards!

Edgelord
At lv1, Bulk can seal up to 3 cards in your hand, making them unplayable for the next 5 turns. Generally for sealed cards, you can:

    Discard the card as usual
    Ignore the seal by transforming the card with Sublimation
    Unseal by returning it to deck, e.g. with Transcode
    Unseal by resetting its status with the Speed Charge skill
    Duplicate an unsealed version with the Double skill

At lv3, up to 5 cards is sealed each time. And at lv5, all cards in hand can be sealed, which is super annoying. The Clock Freeze skill becomes particularly helpful here.

Degenerate Cheese Strat :

Domestic Violence
In your sparring match with Rita, she has the ability to:

    Increase all cards' cost by 1
    Decrease all cards' cost by 1
    Set 1 random card to Fragile, its cost to 0, and damage to 0

Basically each time you play a Red Card, she changes what ability she would use. So say if you only want her to use the cost-reduction ability, you would have to hit her 2 times every 3 cards.

Her HP stays at 300 regardless of BIAS, but she does have a "2nd phase", starting at the 3rd sparring match and at BIAS 13+. From there, she seems to pick a random ability from below with varying duration:

    Applies 10 seals to 3 cards
    Adds 3 "Regret" cards to deck
    Draws 3 cards from deck
    Return all hand to deck

The last one is the most threatening since it leaves you with no card to play. Simplest solution is to have a skill that can draw more cards (e.g. Drizzle), but you can also Clock Freeze her, and then land your already buffed up cards before she can use any ability.

Not D-rex
I only recall seeing when I'm at BIAS 16, so I assume defeating Azwen at lv3 is needed. In any case, his ability is that every 3 turns, he lets you choose 1 of the 3 bad things that can happen:

    Obscure information of your cards
    Randomise card costs
    Heal itself for each card played

Picking the same option would make the next effect more detrimental, like it goes from "heal 5 HP" to "10 HP", eventually "99 HP". (see spreadsheet for all possible effects) Anyway, I always pick the first one, since I've already known my deck very well, and can already play them without all the info.

Oh and one more thing - Besides giving more Upgrade Chips than other bosses upon defeat, you also get to pick the same skills prior to the fight. This means even without the Backdoor card, you can still use Double twice!
Quick Achievement Guide
Most achievements are pretty straight forward, but in case you are unsure how to obtain some of these, here's a quick summary:
Recommended ways to get all achievements:

    Win against bosses
    - Win against all bosses at lv5 simultaneously, i.e. BIAS-20
    - Win against the boss in optional path (any BIAS)
    - Win against Rita's 2nd phase (BIAS 13+)
    Defeat the 999 HP scarecrow with the Simple Laevatein build
    - While you're at it, use Homeward Bound on Laevatein
    Fight the 99,999 HP scarecrow with the Infinite Reckless Plan build
    - Play 50 cards in one fight
    - Land an attack that deals 150+ damage (in 1 hit)
    Play Procyon Device 5 times in one battle
    - Homeward Bound can be used to basically duplicate cards
    Grind enough Upgrade Chips to upgrade all cards
    - The optional boss gives 50 Upgrade Chips, instead of the usual 15
    - I always skip the 4th boss since his fight takes too long for me
