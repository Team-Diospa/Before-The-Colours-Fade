Beginner's Questions / FAQ
Q: How does a general run usually go?
Basically you start with one of the available preset deck, and slowly obtain stronger cards to replace the weak ones in the preset. Each battle is a mini-puzzle where you are solving by defeating the enemies, and you get bonus gold for using fewer cards, which is then used to purchase more upgrades before the end of each floor (5th battle).

Q: What's the best deck to play with?
Honestly, you want to test out all 6 decks eventually anyway, and then complete them at all 5 difficulties to reach the ending. The order doesn't really matter. Though, I do recommend doing the decks you find more difficult first, since with the more cards you unlock, the more likely you can get bad luck and couldn't find the cards suitable for the deck.
(For me, I always have trouble with Print Deck)

Q: Should I continue to Floor 3?
Only the first time clearing provides rewards. But if you want to test out how far your current deck can go, there is no stopping you from attempting it. You'll likely each max level naturally anyway, so there is no need to grind these for the extra EXP.

Q: What does the Mysterious Character for clearing Level 5 do?
Practically nothing.

Q: What does the Treasure for clearing after reaching the ending do?
Also nothing! It does not unlock anything new besides looking nice in your collection.

Q: What is this card that I am missing in Field Guide?
Assuming you have already level up your EXP to max, and fiinish all 30 levels of decks, you are likely missing either a Printable card or a "Dud" card.
- For "Dud", see the "Save-Scum" section
- For printable, see the "All Printable Cards" section

You can use this spreadsheet page[docs.google.com] to check which specific card you are missing.
More General Tips
Q: How does Mana work?

    If card has Mana cost, deduct from current Mana accordingly
    If card is Drain, gain the listed amount in description
    Otherwise, +1 Mana whenever a card is played

Also note that Mana is capped at 10. So if you have cards like Mana Sword, make sure not to right-click it too many times, since anything above 10 won't be usable at all.

Q: How do I deal with enemies with Metal?
Multi-hit attacks allow you to shave multiple HP at once, and residue damage from Shock attacks can also hit it. And lastly, status effect would work wonder here, where Bleed and Poison practically doubles the damage each, and Doom bypasses it entirely.

Also worth noting that Metal is an aliment after all. Beside transferral with a certain relic, if you happen to be using Print Deck, you can either remove it or attempt to win by rulebook with some other aliment.

Q: Any interesting tips and strategies?

    Paying 100g for cards can still be profitable, e.g. extra Drain for using Endless
    Negative attack values heal enemies, can be useful for fine-tuning HP for Reckoning
    Zero attack value still counts as an attack, triggering Bleed and Curse effects
    Overwrite can be very good on cards like Satchel to generate multiple cards
    Overwrite can have negative effects on cards like Ice, where the ATK is reduced faster
    Demi is especially strong against bosses, which can have 50+ HP at times
    - This counts as an attack, with Highlight you can also deal the same big damage again
    Reckoning requires enemy to be at specific HP when the attack lands
    - If you grants it AoE or multi-hit, you only need 1 hit to land at specific HP
    Holy Grail and Split can duplicate a treasure chest to generate more cards
    Some cards can let to effortlessly win in certain deck (see "Deck Overviews")
    If you use Eraser on cards like Scartch, it can reward you with money after a battle
    You can save-scum if you really want a specific set of cards (see below)

Power Deck 101
Power Deck is simple and strong. IMO the core strategy is usual this - Deal big damage, then spread it to multiple enemies.

Just its starting kit is pretty sufficient. You can do at least 10 damage with Duplicate on Discard, then you can use Megaphone on Highlight to spread it to multiple enemies. You want to prioritise upgrading Megaphone to hit more enemies at once, and the reduced Highlight cost can be useful if you have other cards requiring Mana.

Demi is also particularly strong on this deck against late-game enemies. More specifcially, if you use it on a 30 HP enemy, your Highlight can now deal the same amount of damage. Better yet, you can double it with Duplicate, then buy a Highlight card to spread it out!

Assassin Deck 101
Assassin Deck comes with a Mana debuff, which means you want to rely less on cards with Mana cost, at least early on. With the free 1-ATK card that this deck generates, Mana card can actually be somewhat more viable than in other decks under circumstances.

Anyway, all 3 status aliments are good with this deck, since you can deal bonus damage using the otherwise weak Scratch card it generates. Moreover, the Claws synergise especially well because you usually get 2 before you're out of cards, making Claws a 8-DMG card without much downside.

Ice is also good for stalling, both for Mana regeneration and for more claws to appear. Combo Kill also somewhat usable for preserving the card (either for money or mana), but it's somewhat difficult to pull off.

Mana Deck 101
Mana Deck comes with many strong cards by default as well. Just with Drain you usually can get enough Mana for other cards, and those tend to be quite strong.

Combine Deck 101
For Combine Deck, usually by combining a Combo Kill, Wind, and Thunder, it's enough to defeat at least one enemy per cast for the early half of the game, allowing you to recast often until all foes are down.

Later on, you may combine multi-hit moves (e.g. Combo Slash) with powerful attacks (e.g. All In), and/or moves that hit multiple enemies (e.g. Flash). Sometimes you may not even need reusable cards to win.

Random Deck 101
This deck consists of mostly just the Random cards, and well, they transform into random cards of the same rarity at the start of each battle.

In a sense, I find this deck to be easier because you don't have to worry about picking good cards too much, on top of the 2 free Mana you start each encounter with. Each encounter is simply "Can I win with this random selection of cards?", and just reroll if not. Not to mention you are much more likely to find upgrades instead of card rewards, so you usually start with better cards than usual in each encounter.

⬇Other Strategies⬇
How to Save-Scum
The game only determine random events as it is happening. If you manually make a local backup for the game, you can just overwrite the .es3 file after exiting to the main menu, while the game is still running.

Since the reward is determined as soon as you enter the room, usually you have to redo the enemy encounter in the room before. One exception is right at a road fork, where you can back up the save there, then upon reload you can simply pick the path to choose whichever cards/relic you are hunting for.

Getting the "Dud" card
This is the only time I've save-scummed, where I had already done multiple runs prior that didn't give me the exact cards needed for creating the "Dud" card. Basically, you would need the following cards:

    Drain
    Endless
    Combine

The easiest way is to start with Print Deck, then save-scum the first 2 cards with the aforementioned methods. And then you can get a Combine card from printing a "cauldon", one of the possible random background objects indoor.

Catapult Chain
If you give the Scope modifier to Catapult. You can simply just spend 100g to draw an extra one, letting you keep hitting the backline with only Catapult until there is only 1 enemy left. This basically guarantees a win, but would be kinda slow, since it's only 2 damage per card, and you have to target an enemy each time.

Shockwave Chain
There is also this combo I randomly stumbled upon. Basically, buff the Shockwave to 5+ ATK, e.g. with Power Up card. Next, you'll need a 2nd Shockwave card, e.g. from Copy File. From there, whenever we use a Shockwave, it would deal enough damage to resurrect the other Shockwave.
